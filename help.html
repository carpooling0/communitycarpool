Help & FAQ

Everything you need to know about Community Carpool — how it works, how your data is protected, and common questions.

📍 How It Works

Three simple steps to your carpool

Community Carpool matches you with colleagues on similar routes — no app required, completely free.

1

Submit your journey

Fill in your name, email, home location, work location, and your preferred maximum distance. That's it — it only takes a minute.

2

We find your matches

Our algorithm compares your route against other registered journeys and calculates a match strength based on how close your pickup and drop-off points are. When a good match is found, you'll receive an email notification.

3

Express interest — then connect

On your matches page, you can mark each match as Interested or Not for me. If both you and your match say Interested, it's a mutual match — contact details are revealed and you can reach out directly to arrange your carpool.

🔒 Privacy & Contact Sharing

Your details are protected

We built the platform with privacy at its core. You stay in control of who sees your information.

👤

Names are masked

Until there's a mutual match, you only see partial names (e.g. "S****"). No full names or contact details are shared until both sides agree.

📧

Email hidden until mutual

Your email address is never shown to another user unless you both express interest in the same match. One-sided interest reveals nothing.

✅

Mutual interest required

Contact details are only revealed when both users independently click "Interested" on the same match — never one-sidedly.

🗑️

Delete any time

You can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time. Deleted accounts are fully removed within 30 days.

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions

How long does it take to find a match?

It depends on how many people in your area have registered. Most people receive their first match notification within a few days of submitting. New matches are checked regularly as more people sign up.

If you haven't heard anything after a couple of weeks, it may mean no one with a similar route has registered yet — but your journey stays active and you'll be notified as soon as a match is found.

How is my contact information protected?

Your full name and email address are never shared with another user unless there is a mutual match — meaning both of you have independently clicked "Interested" on the same match.

Until then, your name is shown only as a masked initial (e.g. "A***") and no email is displayed. You can also decline any match by clicking "Not for me", and that match will be removed from your view.

What is "mutual interest"?

When you and another person are matched, you each independently decide whether to express interest. A mutual match happens when both of you click "Interested" — only then are contact details revealed to both parties.

If only one person expresses interest, nothing is shared. This ensures that no one receives unsolicited contact.

Can I have multiple journeys registered?

Yes! You can submit a new journey at any time. Each submission is treated independently, so you might have one journey for your regular commute and another for a different route. All your journeys and their matches are shown on your matches page.

How do I update or remove my journey?

You can deactivate or pause a journey directly from your matches page — just open the journey and use the deactivate option. This keeps your data but stops you being matched for that journey.

To fully remove a journey or delete your account, use the unsubscribe/deletion option from the email we sent you, or submit a support request and we'll handle it promptly.

What happens to my data if I delete my account?

When you request account deletion, your account is scheduled for permanent removal within 30 days. During this window you can cancel the request via the link in the confirmation email.

Once deleted, all your personal information, journey submissions, and match history are permanently erased from our systems. Data is not recoverable after deletion.
